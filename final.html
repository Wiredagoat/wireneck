Bringing Nature Closer to Urban Living

Nature

Video that shows nature in Manchester

Manchester is home to several beautiful parks and gardens, offering residents and visitors a peaceful escape from city life. One of the most famous is Heaton Park, one of the largest municipal parks in Europe, boasting acres of greenery, woodlands, a boating lake, and even a farm. Other notable green spaces include Fletcher Moss Park & Botanical Gardens, Whitworth Park, and Platt Fields Park. The city is crisscrossed by various rivers and canals, adding to its natural charm. The Bridgewater Canal, for example, is a historic waterway that stretches from Manchester to Liverpool, offering scenic walking and cycling routes along its banks. Manchester has a thriving community gardening scene, with numerous allotments and community gardens scattered throughout the city. These spaces not only provide opportunities for residents to grow their own food but also foster a sense of community and connection to nature.

Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh

❮ ❯

The Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh (RBGE) is a world-renowned center for plant science, horticulture, and education. Located in Edinburgh, Scotland, it covers an area of over 70 acres and is home to an extensive collection of plants from around the world. The garden serves as both a tourist attraction and a research institution, playing a vital role in the conservation and study of plant biodiversity. Its mission includes the conservation of plant species, the advancement of botanical knowledge through research, and the promotion of environmental awareness and education. The garden's diverse collections include alpine plants, tropical palms, orchids, and many more, cultivated across various themed areas and glasshouses.

Indoor Plants

Indoor plants not only add beauty to your home but also improve air quality and reduce stress levels. Low-cost options include spider plants, pothos, and snake plants.

About Us

We are a team passionate about bringing the benefits of nature to urban communities. Our mission is to provide practical and affordable solutions for incorporating greenery into urban living spaces. We organise educational programs and workshops that highlight the benefits of spending time in nature, such as mental health benefits, physical well-being, and environmental awareness. These programs can cover topics like wildlife conservation, gardening, outdoor activities, and sustainable living practices.

What You Should do!

Young adults aged 18 to 25 in Manchester can take various actions to engage with nature, contribute to the community, and promote environmental sustainability. 1.Explore Local Green Spaces: Take time to explore the many parks, nature reserves, and green spaces in and around Manchester. Spend leisure time outdoors, go for walks, have picnics, or simply relax and appreciate the natural beauty. 2.Participate in Outdoor Activities: Engage in outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, jogging, or wildlife spotting. Join local outdoor clubs or groups to meet like-minded individuals and participate in organized outdoor adventures. 3.Volunteer for Environmental Projects: Get involved in volunteering opportunities focused on environmental conservation and sustainability. Participate in community clean-up events, tree planting initiatives, habitat restoration projects, or wildlife monitoring programs. 4.Attend Nature-Based Workshops and Events: Attend workshops, seminars, and events related to nature, environmental education, and outdoor recreation. Learn new skills, gain knowledge about local ecosystems, and connect with experts and enthusiasts in the field.
